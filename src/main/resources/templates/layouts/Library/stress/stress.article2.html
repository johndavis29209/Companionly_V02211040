Stress vs. Anxiety: Understanding the Difference

By John Davis | Published on February 19, 2025

While stress and anxiety are often used interchangeably, they are distinct experiences that affect our mental and physical well-being in different ways.

What is Stress?

Stress is typically a response to an external pressure or demand. It can be acute or chronic and may result from work, relationships, or other life events. When managed properly, stress can motivate us to perform under pressure.

What is Anxiety?

Anxiety, on the other hand, is characterized by persistent worry or fear that is disproportionate to the situation. It often involves physical symptoms such as rapid heartbeat and tension, and can interfere with daily functioning.

Understanding the differences between stress and anxiety is crucial for managing both effectively. By identifying the root causes of your feelings, you can better tailor your strategies to improve your mental health.
