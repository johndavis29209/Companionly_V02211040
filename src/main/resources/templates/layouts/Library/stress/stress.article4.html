The Role of Adaptogens and Nutrition in Stress Management

By John Davis | Published on February 19, 2025

Adaptogens are natural substances that help the body adapt to stress. When combined with a balanced diet, they can play a significant role in stress management by supporting both mental and physical resilience.

What Are Adaptogens?

Adaptogens are active ingredients in certain plants and mushrooms that may impact how your body deals with stress, anxiety and fatigue. Plants and mushrooms provide adaptogenic actions. When consumed, these plants target specific stressors in your body. There are three qualities that plants must have to be an adaptogen:

  • It is non-toxic when taken in normal doses.
  • It helps your body cope with stress.
  • It allows your body to return to balance (homeostasis).
Adaptogens work as a temporary bandage but aren't the solution to long-term stress.

What do adaptogens do to my body?

The goal of taking adaptogens is to return your body back to a state of balance (homeostasis). The herbal action in adaptogens increases or decreases chemical reactions within your body. For example, if you're stressed (elevated cortisol), an adaptogen will respond by reducing cortisol levels. If you experience chronic fatigue with low cortisol levels, an adaptogen will increase the level of cortisol in your body.

The Importance of Nutrition

Good nutrition provides the essential building blocks for maintaining energy and coping with stress. A diet rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants supports the immune system and aids in recovery from stress-related fatigue.

Integrating adaptogens with proper nutrition can enhance your body's natural ability to manage stress and improve your quality of life.

Are adaptogens regulated by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration?

While some adaptogens like ashwagandha and Asian ginseng have a number of studies on their varied actions, supplements are not regulated by FDA, and FDA approval does not equate to safety. If you plan to add plants with adaptogenic qualities to your diet, talk with your healthcare provider to discuss how they could impact your health and if they are right for you.
